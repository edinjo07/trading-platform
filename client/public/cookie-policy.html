01 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They allow websites to recognize your device on subsequent visits, remember your preferences, and provide a more personalized experience.

Similar technologies include:

  • Local Storage: Browser-based key-value storage used to persist app state (e.g., your auth token)
  • Session Storage: Temporary storage cleared when the browser tab is closed
  • IndexedDB: Browser database for larger structured data storage

This policy covers all such technologies that we use on the Platform.

02 How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Authenticating you and keeping you signed in securely
  • Remembering your layout preferences, theme settings, and active watchlists
  • Maintaining your session state across the single-page application
  • Analyzing how users interact with different features of the Platform
  • Detecting and preventing fraudulent or abusive behaviour
  • Improving platform performance and reliability

We do not use cookies for advertising, tracking across third-party websites, or selling data to ad networks.

03 Types of Cookies We Use

🔐

Essential Cookies

Required

These cookies are strictly necessary for the Platform to function. They enable authentication, session management, and security features. The Platform cannot work properly without them. They cannot be disabled without losing access to core features.

⚙️

Functional Cookies

Optional

These cookies remember your preferences and settings across sessions, such as your selected trading pairs, chart layout, sidebar state, and notification preferences. Without them, you'll need to re-configure your settings each visit.

Analytics Cookies

Optional

These help us understand how users interact with the Platform - which features are used most, where users encounter difficulties, and how to prioritize improvements. All analytics data is anonymized and aggregated. No individual user is identifiable in our analytics reports.
